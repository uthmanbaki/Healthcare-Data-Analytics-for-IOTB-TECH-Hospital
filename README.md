# Healthcare-Data-Analytics-for-IOTB-TECH-Hospital
# Executive Summary
The dataset contains 54,966 patient records and shows a hospital with fairly balanced activity across conditions, gender, test outcomes, and admission types. Even so, a few patterns stand out. Patients aged 70+ make up the largest share of admissions. Elective admissions are the most common. Cigna contributes the highest total billing amount, and Diabetes generates the most revenue by condition. The average hospital stay is about 15.5 days.

# Key Questions
1. Which condition generates the highest revenue? 
Diabetes ranks first, producing about ₦236,486,971.10 in billing. It also has one of the strongest average billing levels per case.

2. Which age group visits the hospital most? 
Patients with the group age 66+ lead the dataset with 16,096 admissions of all visits.

3. Which insurance provider contributes the most financially? 
Cigna contributes the most, with total billing of about ₦284,334,099.18.

4. Which admission type is most common? 
Elective admissions are the most common at 18,473 records.

5. Which condition keeps patients in the hospital the longest? 
Asthma has the longest average stay at 15.7 days

# Observations
1. Gender is almost perfectly balanced, with 27,496 male patients and 27,470 female patients. That means the dataset does not lean heavily toward one gender.
2. Test results are also closely balanced: 18,437 abnormal, 18,331 normal, and 18,198 inconclusive cases.
3. The highest admissions year is 2020 with 11,172 records. The 2024 figures are lower, but that is expected because the dataset only runs up to 2024-05-07, so 2024 is incomplete.
4. Admissions and revenue both peak in late summer. August has the highest admission count, followed closely by July, which may indicate a seasonal rise in hospital activity.

# 10 Insights from the Analysis
1.	Older adults are the main patient segment. The 66+ group alone accounts for 12,849 admissions and about 23.3% of total revenue.
2.	Diabetes is the most valuable condition financially, but the revenue gap between conditions is not extreme. That means the hospital's income is spread fairly evenly across case types.
3.	Elective care is slightly ahead of urgent and emergency admissions, so scheduled service lines likely play a major role in hospital throughput.
4.	Cigna is the strongest payer by total billing, which makes it an important insurance partner for revenue stability.
5.	Asthma patients stay the longest on average, so this condition is likely to consume more bed time and nursing resources than other conditions.
6.	Lipitor is the most used medication, but medication usage is very balanced overall; no single drug dominates the pharmacy picture.
7.	The hospital's average length of stay is 15.5 days, which suggests discharge planning needs to stay efficient to protect bed capacity.
8.	A near 50/50 gender split shows the hospital is serving a broad patient mix and does not have a strong gender bias in admissions.
9.	Year 2020 recorded the highest volume and billing, while later years remain consistently high, showing stable demand rather than a one-time spike.
10.	Monthly activity is strongest in July and August, so staffing, pharmacy stock, and bed scheduling should be reinforced during that period.

# Recommendations
1. More beds should be planned for, nurses and pharmacy should stock for the 66+ patient segment, because it is the largest group and likely drives much of the hospital workload.
2. Keep a close commercial relationship with Cigna, since it is the top financial contributor.
3. Review asthma pathways and discharge planning, because longer stays can reduce bed availability and increase operating pressure.
4. Prepare for seasonal demand spikes around July and August by adjusting staffing and supply levels ahead of time.
5. Use Elective admission planning to smooth capacity, since elective cases are the largest admission type and can be scheduled more efficiently than urgent cases.
